id: MS48
name: Gear Too low when accelerating
family: MS
common_co_labels: [MS35, MS36, MS46]

## Definition
On corner exit, the driver is in a lower gear than the expert —
engine RPM is above its torque peak and pulling toward the
limiter where a taller gear would give cleaner acceleration.

## Physics
Below peak power the engine produces more torque per unit RPM
than above it. In too low a gear, the engine spends time at
higher RPM but lower torque per RPM, and on RWD cars the
combination of high torque-multiplied-by-gear-ratio plus narrow
torque band can cause traction issues (MS46).

## Telemetry signature
- Engine RPM at throttle-on near or above peak power compared to
  expert in same corner.
- Driven-axle slip more prone to spike (excess wheel torque).
- Acceleration trace plateaus earlier in the gear than expert.
- Often a downstream effect of MS35 (early downshift).

## Engineer interpretation
You picked too short a gear. The fix is usually upstream — the
downshift sequence put you here. Plan the gear for the apex and
exit; the gear you arrive in is the gear you exit in.

## Remedies
- Use one fewer downshift if the corner allows.
- Verify your gear plan corner-by-corner.
- If you keep landing too low through a particular corner, the
  rev-match on the last downshift might be pulling the engine
  too high.
