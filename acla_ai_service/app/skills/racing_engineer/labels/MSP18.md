## Definition
Brake pressure falls from peak to zero more gradually than the
expert's trail-off — the driver lingers on the pedal too long
through the corner.

## Physics
Carrying brake too far into the corner keeps front load high past
the apex, which is where the front should be giving grip back to
the rear. Extended brake also drains exit speed: every joule of
energy you scrub past the optimal release point is energy you have
to put back in on the next straight.

## Telemetry signature
- Time from peak brake to zero brake longer than expert.
- Brake pressure non-zero past the expert's release point.
- Apex speed often lower than expert (over-slowed).
- Throttle-on point delayed (MSP21).

## Engineer interpretation
You're using the brake as a security blanket through the apex.
Trail-braking is great; trail-loitering is not. The pedal should
be at zero by the time the car settles into the apex unless the
corner is unusually long.

## Remedies
- Target zero brake at the apex.
- Match the expert's release shape — if the expert was off the
  brake by entry, so should you be.
- If the slow release is what's keeping the car settled, the
  trail-off ramp may be the wrong shape (steep at first then
  long) — work on the shape, not the duration.
