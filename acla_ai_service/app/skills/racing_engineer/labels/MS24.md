name: Brake applied too slowly
family: MS

## Definition
Brake-pressure ramp-up is more gradual than the expert's — the
driver builds pressure slowly when the car is asking for a decisive
hit.

## Physics
A slow ramp-up means front load arrives slowly, so the front tyres
take longer to reach their optimal slip and slow the car. The
brake phase ends up longer than needed; lap time leaks because
top-of-straight speed was wasted on a gentle scrub.

## Telemetry signature
- Brake derivative (rate of change) lower than expert at brake-
  onset.
- Brake-pressure curve has a long shallow leading edge.
- Brake duration extended (MS32 often follows).
- Top speed normal but apex speed acceptable — the time was lost
  in the ramp.

## Engineer interpretation
Brake isn't a dial, it's a switch you modulate. Hit it firmly, then
shape the trail-off. A clean ramp gets to the plateau quickly and
gives you more pedal travel to work with for trail-braking.

## Remedies
- Build pressure in ~0.2 seconds, not 0.4–0.5.
- The first half of the brake should be assertive; shape the
  second half.
- If you ramp slowly because you're afraid of lock-up, that's a
  separate skill — practise threshold braking in a low-stakes
  corner first.
