## Definition
The car enters the corner from a wider lateral position than the
expert reference — sitting outside the expert line at turn-in.

## Physics
A wide entry means the car has further to travel to reach the apex,
so the line works out either through an extra steering input
(scrubbing speed) or an early apex with a forced wide exit. A
genuinely wide entry sometimes opens up a faster line if the apex
and exit work — but most often it's a defensive line that just
loses time.

## Telemetry signature
- Lateral track position at turn-in outside expert reference.
- Steering angle larger than expert through entry.
- May produce an early apex (MS7) and wide exit (MS16) if the
  geometry collapses.
- Apex speed often below expert (extra steering ate the speed).

## Engineer interpretation
You came in wider than the line wanted. If you did it on purpose
to defend, fine — but commit to the geometry of the wider line all
the way through. Half-measures (wide entry, expert apex, expert
exit) make the corner shape worse than either consistent line.

## Remedies
- Aim turn-in for the expert's reference, not your comfort line.
- If defending, plan the *whole* corner as a wider line — accept
  the slightly slower apex.
- If the wide entry is from carrying too much speed into the
  brake zone (MS29), fix the lift point first.
