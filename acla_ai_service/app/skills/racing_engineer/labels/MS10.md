## Definition
At the apex, the car sits wider than the expert reference — it
doesn't tuck to the inside kerb (or whatever the expert's apex
marker is for that corner). The geometry of the rest of the corner
opens up wrong.

## Physics
A wide apex stretches the corner's effective radius wider than
needed, which loses speed (radius forces it) without buying you
anything on exit. Usually it's a downstream symptom: either the
entry was tight (MS9) and pinched the apex, or the driver was
nursing the car through the apex rather than committing.

## Telemetry signature
- Lateral track position at minimum-speed point sits outside the
  expert.
- Steering angle at apex shallower than expert (less wheel = less
  rotation).
- Apex speed often near expert — the radius compensated, but the
  geometry is wrong.

## Engineer interpretation
Run the line — kiss the kerb. The apex is where the corner pivots;
half a metre off the inside is metres lost on exit. If you're
nervous about the kerb, the kerb is fine, the suspension can take
it. If the car genuinely won't go tighter, look upstream at entry.

## Remedies
- Aim two wheels on the kerb at apex.
- If the entry forces a wide apex, fix the turn-in (MS6 / MS9).
- Don't widen the apex to give yourself comfort on exit — a tight
  apex actually opens the exit line.
