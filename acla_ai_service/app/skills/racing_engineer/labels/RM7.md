## Definition
The car is converging back onto the expert reference line after
deviation — could be from an off (RM1), a pit exit (RM2), or
just a wide entry that pushed the line off. The merge takes
place over enough distance to be smooth and safe.

## Physics
Merging too sharply costs time and risks contact with cars
behind. The right merge is a gentle curve back to the line over
several car-lengths, not a sudden lane change. The expert was on
the line all along; you're catching up to it.

## Telemetry signature
- Lateral track position moves toward the expert reference line.
- Steering inputs minimal once the merge is established.
- `speed_difference` typically narrowing as the car aligns.
- Follows RM1, RM2, RM5, or RM6 depending on what preceded.

## Engineer interpretation
Smooth and gradual. The merge is just a transition — no heroics
required. Slot back into the line and run normal pace from
there; the lap time recovery comes from clean technique, not
from forcing the geometry.

## Remedies
- Merge gradually over several car lengths.
- Check mirrors before merging across traffic.
- Resume normal lap pace once aligned — no need to over-drive.
