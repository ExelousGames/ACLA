## Definition
Driver takes a shortcut inside the official racing surface —
inside-kerb cuts that go beyond what the track-limits rules
permit, or full diagonals across chicanes. The classifier flags
it; the stewards will too.

## Physics
Cutting reduces the distance the car has to travel and often the
corner radius — so the apparent lap time looks faster, but the
penalty is either a slow-down warning, a lap time deleted, or
post-session disqualification. In iRacing/ACC etc. the slow-down
is enforced live.

## Telemetry signature
- Lateral track position crosses the inside white line by more
  than the kerb allows.
- Wheels-on-track count drops below the configured threshold for
  the corner.
- Lap time before any penalty looks fast (because it is — illegally).

## Engineer interpretation
Don't. It's not a coaching point — it's a rules point. Cutting
will either be undone by a slow-down or end your race. Run the
proper line; if the car needs more inside, your line is wrong, not
the track.

## Remedies
- Respect the inside kerb / white line — wheels at the edge, not
  beyond.
- If a corner consistently invites a cut, your entry line is too
  wide (MSP33).
- Watch back the in-game replay or external footage — sometimes
  the cut isn't intentional and points to a line problem.
