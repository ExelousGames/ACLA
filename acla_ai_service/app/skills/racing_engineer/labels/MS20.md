name: Initiate throttle too early

## Definition
Throttle application begins before the expert's throttle-on point
— the driver opens the gas while the car is still loaded with
steering input.

## Physics
Asking the rear axle for power while it's also working laterally
overloads the tyres. On RWD cars this often pushes the rear into
power oversteer; on FWD cars the front loses grip and you get
understeer (MS47) as weight transfers backward off the front. Either
way exit speed drops.

## Telemetry signature
- Throttle-onset position ahead of expert reference.
- Steering still significantly loaded at throttle-onset.
- Front or rear slip spikes at throttle-onset (axle depending on
  drivetrain).
- Exit trajectory drifts wide (MS16).

## Engineer interpretation
Patience pays. The throttle is part of the corner exit, not a
shortcut to it. Wait for the wheel to start coming back to centre
before you open the throttle — that's the car telling you it's
ready for power.

## Remedies
- Tie throttle-on to steering unwind, not to a track position.
- Delay by half a beat — feel the wheel go light first.
- If the rear lets go even with proper timing, the apex may be too
  tight (MS9) — slight wider apex frees the rear.
