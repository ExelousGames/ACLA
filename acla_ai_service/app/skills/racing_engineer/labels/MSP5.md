## Definition
Brake application begins before the expert's reference point. Costs
time on the preceding straight — you're slowing down when you could
still be accelerating.

## Physics
Every metre of brake before the optimal point is a metre you stopped
accelerating early. Top speed at brake-onset is lower than it could
be, and the corner doesn't need the extra scrub — so the brake
phase is longer and softer (MSP32, MSP24) to compensate. The driver
"feels safe" but the lap time pays.

## Telemetry signature
- Brake onset earlier than expert by more than a few metres.
- Top speed before the brake zone slightly below expert's.
- Brake pressure trace looks like a long shallow ramp instead of a
  decisive build.
- Apex speed often equal to or higher than expert (you scrubbed
  enough — and then some).

## Engineer interpretation
Confidence problem more than skill problem. The car will brake later
than you think — work the reference point back in increments. Two
metres per attempt, not ten.

## Remedies
- Move the brake reference 2–5 m later, lap by lap.
- Use a hard, fixed marker (board, kerb, sponsor sign) rather than
  eyeballing a track feature.
- Trust the car — it has more pad and more grip than your panic
  brake assumes.
