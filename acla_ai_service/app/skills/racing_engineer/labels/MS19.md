## Definition
Peak throttle on exit doesn't reach what the expert reached. The
driver lifts or modulates when the car can take full throttle.

## Physics
Under-throttling on exit is one of the easiest places to find lap
time — it doesn't usually require any change in confidence at
entry, just commitment on the way out. The cost compounds: lost
exit speed bleeds onto the next straight and bleeds again into the
next corner's entry speed budget.

## Telemetry signature
- Peak throttle on exit lower than expert peak.
- Throttle trace plateaus below 100% where expert goes flat.
- Speed delta vs expert grows from exit onto the following straight.
- `expert_optimal_throttle` shows the expert was flat where the
  driver was modulating.

## Engineer interpretation
You're leaving lap time on the table on the easiest part of the
corner. The exit phase is where commitment shows. If the car
isn't punishing you for full throttle, go flat — and if it is,
that's a useful signal too (wrong apex, wrong exit line, or a
setup issue).

## Remedies
- Commit to 100% throttle once the steering starts unwinding.
- Compare to `expert_optimal_throttle` — if the expert was flat,
  there's no excuse.
- If the car oversteers under full throttle, look at apex tightness
  (MS10) and rear stability.
