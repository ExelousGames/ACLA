## Definition
Exit line stays tighter than the expert reference — the driver
doesn't use the available track on exit, hugging the inside instead
of opening out to the white line.

## Physics
A narrow exit means the steering is held in longer than it needs to
be, holding the car on a tighter radius right when the radius
should be opening up. Lateral grip is being burned on holding a
line instead of accepting throttle, so exit speed suffers.

## Telemetry signature
- Lateral track position on exit sits inside the expert reference
  line.
- Steering unwinds later or more slowly than expert.
- Throttle-on may be timid (pair with MSP21) because the wheel is
  still loaded.
- Exit speed lower than expert.

## Engineer interpretation
Use the track. The white line is there for a reason — running short
of it on exit is the same as leaving lap time on the table. Trust
the car to take the wider line; the geometry rewards it.

## Remedies
- Aim for the outside white line at exit, not mid-track.
- Eyes well past the corner so the hands let the wheel unwind.
- If the wheel won't unwind (front grip holding the line), it may
  be MSP47 territory — front is overloaded.
