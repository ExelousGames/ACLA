## Definition
The car returns to the track surface after going off — onto
gravel, grass, run-off, or beyond the kerb. The recovery segment
covers the re-entry and the rejoining of the racing line.

## Physics
Recovery has two costs: the lost speed during the off (low-grip
surface, lifted throttle to regain control) and the lateral time
re-merging into traffic safely. The expert never had to make this
move on a clean lap; the time you lose here is added to whatever
mistake caused the off.

## Telemetry signature
- Wheels-on-track count returns to full as the car re-joins.
- Lateral track position moves from outside the surface back
  inside.
- Brief throttle modulation while traction returns.
- RM7 typically follows as the car aligns back to the expert line.

## Engineer interpretation
The off is in the past — focus on a clean rejoin. The biggest
mistake after going off is rushing back to the racing line and
losing the car again or interfering with traffic. Stabilise
first, rejoin second, get back on pace third.

## Remedies
- Get the car straight before re-applying full throttle.
- Check mirrors — rejoining in front of a faster car is a
  flag-worthy offence.
- Don't try to make the next corner instantly — finish the
  recovery first.
