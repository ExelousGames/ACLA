## Definition
Time or position loss caused by an *interaction* with a close opponent —
not by a technical execution error on an empty piece of track.
Sub-labels describe the specific kind of interaction failure: a pass
attempt that didn't stick, or a defensive line that got broken.

## When this applies
A racing mistake applies when a close opponent is present and the
interaction outcome is adverse — the player tried to pass and didn't,
or got passed while trying to hold. If no close opponent is present,
the section is a practice mistake, not a racing one; the technical
execution error stands on its own.

## How to read a racing-mistake chain
A racing mistake rarely arrives alone — the player's trace usually
shows practice-mistake symptoms too (a late brake, a tight entry)
because the *cause* of the failed interaction is the optimistic move
the player tried to make. The job is to separate the two: the racing
sub-label captures the decision, the co-occurring practice sub-labels
capture how it broke down technically. Coach the decision first, the
execution second.

## Engineer interpretation
The driver got into a position with another car and the position cost
them. Don't moralise the move — every overtake attempt that fails or
defense that breaks once worked for someone in the same spot. Identify
whether the right call was *don't try here* (wrong place) or *try here
but commit harder / earlier* (right place, wrong execution), and give
one concrete next-time rule.
