## Definition
The driver is recovering from a prior mistake or off-track and
merging back onto the expert line — the segment isn't a mistake in
itself, but it's also not a clean lap. Sub-labels describe the
specific recovery: off-track, pit exit, brake-earlier correction,
and so on.

## When this applies
After a mistake segment that pushed the driver off-line, the next
segment (or two) is typically a recovery while the car returns to its
reference. It also applies after pit exits, off-track excursions, and
after avoiding incidents.

## How to read recoveries in a session
Recovery segments are normal and not bad on their own — every session
has some. The interesting question is what *led* to the recovery. A
mistake-then-recovery pair is a story: the driver made a mistake and
dealt with it. A clean session keeps recoveries rare and short.

## Engineer interpretation
Don't coach on the recovery directly — coach on what caused the need
for recovery. The driver knows they went off; what they need is a fix
for the upstream mistake so the next attempt doesn't need a recovery
at all. The exception: long recovery segments where the driver lost
more time on the recovery than on the original mistake — then suggest
a faster recovery technique (later brake, smaller correction, etc.).
