## Definition
The driver is matching the expert reference closely — line, brake
points, throttle application, apex speed are all within tolerance.
There's no mistake to label and no recovery to make.

## When this applies
The default for clean segments. The classifier emits EA when no other
specific label applies and the time-difference-to-expert is flat or
closing.

## How to read EA in a session
EA is the goal. A high proportion of EA segments across a session
means the driver is consistent and on pace. A driver with 80% EA and
20% MSP is a faster driver to coach than one with 50% EA and 50%
mixed — focus on the few mistakes rather than rebuilding fundamentals.

## Engineer interpretation
Nothing to fix here. If the driver asks "how was that lap?" and most
of the segments are EA, the honest answer is "clean — that's the
benchmark, do it again." Don't invent feedback when there isn't any.
The trap is filling silence with generic advice; resist it.
