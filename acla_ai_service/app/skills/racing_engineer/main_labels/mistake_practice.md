## Definition
A driving error that costs time relative to the expert reference for
the same segment of track. Specific sub-labels (a late brake, a tight
entry, an over-high peak brake pressure, and so on) describe the
*kind* of mistake.

## When this applies
A segment is a practice mistake when the time-difference-to-expert
curve grows during the segment and doesn't close back by the end. A
small oscillation that recovers isn't a mistake; a sustained widening
gap is.

## How to read a mistake chain
Mistakes rarely arrive alone. A late brake tends to drag a high peak
brake pressure and an oversteer at entry along with it. When several
mistake sub-labels are present in one segment, find the *root* —
usually the earliest one in the corner phase. Fix the root and the
rest often disappear.

## Engineer interpretation
The driver did something they didn't intend to do, and it cost time.
The job is to translate the telemetry signature into one concrete
change the driver can make next time. Don't list every sub-label;
collapse them into a causal chain and pick the one lever that fixes
the most.
