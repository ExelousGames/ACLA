## Definition
The duration spent at peak brake is shorter than the expert's —
the brake plateau is too brief, often because the driver releases
the pedal almost as soon as the peak is reached.

## Physics
The peak-brake plateau is where most of the speed-scrubbing
happens. A short plateau means the brake is doing less of its
total work, so the trail phase has to compensate with a higher
average pressure or the apex speed runs high. Either way the
corner doesn't get the full benefit of the brake.

## Telemetry signature
- Time spent above ~90% of peak brake shorter than expert.
- Brake pressure curve looks like a sharp spike rather than a
  brief plateau.
- May pair with initiate brake release too early (release too early) or release brake too quickly (released too
  fast).
- Apex speed sometimes higher than expert (under-scrubbed).

## Engineer interpretation
Stay on it. A clean brake is a brief but deliberate plateau —
held just long enough to scrub the speed before the trail-off
takes over. Pulling off too quickly cuts the work short.

## Remedies
- Aim to hold peak for the same distance the expert does (varies
  by corner — short for fast corners, longer for slow ones).
- If the brake feels overwhelming at peak, the peak is too high
  (highest brake pressure too high) — lower the peak so you can sustain it.
