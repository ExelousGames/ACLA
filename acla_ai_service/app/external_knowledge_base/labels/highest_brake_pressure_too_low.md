## Definition
Peak brake pressure falls noticeably below the expert's peak — the
driver under-presses the pedal for the corner. Often pairs with an
early brake (initiate brake too early) and a long brake duration (highest brake length too long).

## Physics
A soft peak means less weight transferred to the front, less front
load, and less grip to scrub speed in the same distance. The driver
compensates by braking earlier and longer — slower lap time even
when the apex speed ends up correct.

## Telemetry signature
- Peak brake pressure below expert peak by more than ~5 percentage
  points.
- Brake-pressure trace is a low plateau rather than a clean spike.
- Brake duration longer than expert (highest brake length too long).
- Top speed before the brake zone slightly below expert (initiate brake too early
  upstream).

## Engineer interpretation
You're treating the brake like a dimmer switch. Modern brakes are
designed to take 85–95% peak comfortably — under-using them just
trades time for the illusion of control. Build to the same peak as
the expert; the rest of the braking technique follows.

## Remedies
- Push the peak — aim for 85–90% on entry.
- Keep the brake-onset point the same, just go in firmer.
- If the car feels twitchy at higher peak, that's highest brake pressure too high territory
  (too high) — the goal is a stable plateau, not the maximum
  available pressure.
