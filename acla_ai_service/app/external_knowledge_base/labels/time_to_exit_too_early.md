## Definition
Driver starts unwinding the steering and opening the throttle
earlier than expert — exits the corner before the apex has been
properly resolved. Lose the back of the corner in a hurry.

## Physics
Opening the line too early hands the rear-axle grip back to the
driver before the lateral load has finished transferring. The car
runs out of corner before it runs out of grip — the result is
usually a wide-exit understeer (exit trajectory too wide) or, with too much throttle,
power oversteer (oversteering at exit).

## Telemetry signature
- Steering input starts unwinding earlier than expert at the same
  position.
- Throttle-on point ahead of expert's reference.
- Exit trajectory drifts wide of the expert line.
- If oversteering at exit fires alongside, the rear is paying for the impatience.

## Engineer interpretation
You're trying to finish the corner before the car has. Patience is
the lever — wait for the steering to start unwinding *itself* under
load before you feed power. The car will tell you when it's ready,
your hands shouldn't.

## Remedies
- Hold the wheel a beat longer through the apex.
- Delay throttle-on by half a count.
- If you keep rushing the exit, slow your eye-line down — looking
  too far past the corner pulls the hands with it.
